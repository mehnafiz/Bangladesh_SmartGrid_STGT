\title{A Correlation-Aware Graph Transformer for Joint Regional Load and Operational Stress Forecasting in Bangladesh}

\input{authors}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Day-ahead grid planning requires both regional load forecasts and view of system-wide operational pressure, yet these are regularly produced by individual tools. In Bangladesh, daily records from nine divisions present evening-peak load together with reserve, limitation, and shedding fields, which produces joint forecasting a realistic fit. We build a parallel-Fusion Spatio Temporal Graph Transformer (PF-STGT) that interprets a seven-day lookback with a train-estimated correlation graph and predicts regional demand and a restricted Operational Stress Index (OSI) from joint encoding and task-specific heads. On a held-out test year, correlation-graph configuration S2 provides the highest-performing joint outcomes in our suite: macro demand MAE 88.65~MW and OSI $R^2$ 0.745, with remarkable demand enhancement over the historical hybrid reference (median $\Delta$MAE $-$5.43~MW, $p = 5.5 \times 10^{-5}$). Ablation supports correlation adjacency over geographical or hybrid graphs and illustrates a trade-off among demand-only accuracy and joint stress output. Post-hoc attribution emphasizes limitation and calendar coalitions, with only moderate consistency across methods. All outcomes are from offline chronological assessment on Bangladesh division-level data; field execution is left to future work. 
\end{abstract}
