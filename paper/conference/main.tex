\title{A Correlation-Aware Graph Transformer for Joint Regional Load and Operational Stress Forecasting in Bangladesh}

\input{authors}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Day-ahead grid planning needs both regional load forecasts and a view of system-wide operational pressure, yet these are often produced by separate tools. In Bangladesh, daily records from nine divisions report evening-peak load together with limitation, reserve, and shedding fields, which makes joint forecasting a natural fit. We build a Parallel-Fusion Spatio-Temporal Graph Transformer (PF-STGT) that reads a seven-day lookback with a train-estimated correlation graph and predicts regional demand and a bounded Operational Stress Index (OSI) from shared encoding and task-specific heads. On a held-out test year, correlation-graph configuration S2 gives the best joint results in our suite: macro demand MAE 88.65~MW and OSI $R^2$ 0.745, with significant demand improvement over the historical hybrid reference (median $\Delta$MAE $-$5.43~MW, $p = 5.5 \times 10^{-5}$). Ablations favour correlation adjacency over geographical or hybrid graphs and show a trade-off between demand-only accuracy and joint stress output. Post-hoc attribution highlights limitation and calendar coalitions, with only partial agreement across methods. All results are from offline chronological evaluation on Bangladesh division-level data; field deployment is left to future work.
\end{abstract}
