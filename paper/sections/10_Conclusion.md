# 10 Conclusion

Day-ahead electricity planning in developing-economy national grids requires synchronised visibility over regional load and system-wide operational pressure, yet forecasting practice often treats demand prediction and stress monitoring as separate pipelines. This study addressed that coupling by investigating whether a single spatio-temporal deep learning framework can jointly forecast next-day regional evening-peak demand and a graph-level Operational Stress Index from shared historical operational records on the Bangladesh national power system \cite{Hossain2025BangladeshSmartGrid}. The research objective was to design, empirically validate, and reproducibly freeze a correlation-aware multi-task forecasting framework under chronological evaluation discipline, with explicit comparison against classical, recurrent, and graph-convolution baselines and systematic ablation of graph priors, architectural components, and task formulation.

The proposed Correlation-Aware Multi-Task Forecasting Framework is implemented as a Parallel-Fusion Spatio-Temporal Graph Transformer (PF-STGT). The model ingests a seven-day lookback of node-level and national context features over nine administrative divisions, conditions representations on a train-estimated correlation graph, and decodes parallel regional demand and system-wide stress outputs through shared spatio-temporal encoding and task-specific heads. A repaired multi-task training protocol balances demand and stress objectives so that joint learning remains stable rather than collapsing to a single-task solution. The final frozen configuration—correlation-graph PF-STGT with full temporal capacity and multi-task heads—was selected through an evidence-locked programme spanning benchmark comparison, configuration ablation, architecture simplification, error geography, and post-hoc attribution, rather than through design intent alone.

The completed evaluation establishes several substantive contributions. First, the study demonstrates that graph-transformer multi-task learning can deliver operationally coupled day-ahead load and stress forecasts from one checkpoint on a chronologically held-out national timeline, extending spatio-temporal energy forecasting beyond load-only benchmarks to limitation-aware stress indices derived from daily grid records. Second, empirical ablation shows that predictive inter-regional coupling in this setting is better served by data-driven correlation adjacency than by geographical or hybrid graph priors imported without dataset-specific justification, while temporal encoding remains consequential once the correlation prior is adopted. Third, the work documents a genuine demand–stress trade-off space within the configuration suite: demand-only training attains a lower megawatt error ceiling, whereas multi-task training enables informative stress outputs at a modest demand offset relative to that ceiling, positioning the frozen model as the preferred joint rather than single-task optimum. Fourth, regional diagnostics reveal a heterogeneous but stable error geography in which peripheral divisions are comparatively well served while the national load centre dominates absolute residual mass—a pattern that persists across model families and informs how national summaries should be read. Fifth, multi-method post-hoc attribution supplies coalition-level, spatial, and temporal transparency for both tasks, with explicit reporting of partial cross-method and dual-path discordance rather than treating any single explanation map as physical validation.

Scientifically, the study contributes a reproducible modelling chain on a previously unreported Bangladesh division-level smart-grid case, responding to geographic and methodological gaps identified in the reviewed literature on graph-based load forecasting, transformer temporal modelling, multi-task energy learning, and explainability in power systems \cite{Musbah2025LoadForecasting,Baur2024XAIReview}. The evidence supports correlation-graph PF-STGT multi-task learning as a viable paradigm where limitation dynamics and inter-regional load coupling coexist in operational data, and it cautions that graph priors, task weighting, and architectural simplification must be empirically tuned rather than assumed from domain intuition or transferred from residential and microgrid settings. The contribution is bounded to the evaluated national context and frozen internal benchmark suite; it advances internal rigour and case specificity rather than claiming universal architectural dominance or external state-of-the-art superiority.

For power-system practice, the principal implication is feasibility: a single forecasting service may supply paired regional demand trajectories and a national stress scalar for day-ahead review from the same input window, which could assist planning workflows that presently reconcile disconnected indicators after inference. Operators may treat macro summaries as routine briefings while reporting the principal load centre separately, reviewing limitation and calendar input channels highlighted in error and attribution analyses, and presenting gradient-based and permutation-based explanation views distinctly where methods disagree. These implications are planning diagnostics grounded in offline accuracy and transparency evidence; they do not constitute demonstrated improvements in grid reliability, economic outcome, or deployment success.

In sum, this work delivers an empirically frozen Correlation-Aware Multi-Task Forecasting Framework that jointly models regional demand and operational stress on Bangladesh national grid data through correlation-graph PF-STGT learning, supported by a transparent evaluation record from task formulation through architecture selection and post-hoc analysis. The study shows that coupled day-ahead load-and-stress forecasting is achievable as a single-model service when graph structure, multi-task calibration, division-level monitoring, and explanation reporting are co-designed with the evidence collected. The conclusions stand as a disciplined contribution to spatio-temporal energy forecasting in developing-economy settings—informative for researchers and practitioners who require reproducible joint forecasts and interpretable model behaviour, and appropriately qualified as a completed offline evaluation rather than an operational field trial.
