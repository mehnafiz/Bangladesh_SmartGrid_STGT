\subsection{Explainability}

Post-hoc attribution \cite{Lundberg2017SHAP,Sundararajan2017IntegratedGradients} on the frozen S2 checkpoint applies grouped GradientSHAP, permutation importance, and spatial attention export without retraining (Experiment~04).

\noindent\textbf{Spatial attention.}
Fig.~\ref{fig:7} visualises mean spatial attention on the train-estimated correlation graph, averaged over 24 case studies. Attribution mass concentrates on Dhaka (340.36), Rajshahi, and Khulna; peripheral divisions receive lower mass. The Spearman correlation between learned attention and frozen adjacency is $\rho{=}0.422$, indicating moderate alignment between data-driven graph structure and learned spatial coupling.

\noindent\textbf{Coalition attributions.}
Fig.~\ref{fig:6} summarises grouped GradientSHAP magnitudes ($|\phi|$) on coalition registry G1--G11 (validation $n{=}20$). \noindent\textbf{Cross-method check.}
Dual-path stress comparison agrees in 13 of 24 case studies (52.2\%). Demand gradient and permutation rankings disagree ($\rho{=}{-}0.564$), whereas stress rankings partially align ($\rho{=}0.366$). Attributions describe model behaviour on the evaluation sample, not physical causation.
