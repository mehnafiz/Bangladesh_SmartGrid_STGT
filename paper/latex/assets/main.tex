\begin{abstract}
Day-ahead planning in developing-economy national grids requires synchronised visibility over regional electricity demand and system-wide operational pressure, yet many pipelines treat load prediction and stress monitoring separately. On the Bangladesh national power system, daily records across nine administrative divisions co-report evening-peak load with limitation, reserve, and shedding indicators, motivating coupled rather than isolated forecasting. This study investigates whether a spatio-temporal model can jointly forecast next-day regional demand and a graph-level Operational Stress Index from shared historical inputs under chronological evaluation. The proposed Correlation-Aware Multi-Task Forecasting Framework is implemented as a Parallel-Fusion Spatio-Temporal Graph Transformer (PF-STGT). The model encodes division-level and national context over a fixed lookback window, conditions representations on a train-estimated correlation graph, and decodes regional demand and a bounded system-wide stress scalar through shared spatio-temporal encoding and task-specific heads, with a repaired multi-task loss protocol that stabilises joint learning. Evaluation on a frozen chronologically held-out test year comprises classical, recurrent, and graph-convolution benchmarks; graph and architecture ablations; regional error analysis; and multi-method post-hoc attribution. Within the evaluated Bangladesh setting, the selected correlation-graph configuration attains the strongest joint demand-and-stress credentials in the internal suite, with statistically supported demand improvement over the historical hybrid reference and comparatively informative OSI outputs relative to stress-capable baselines. Ablation evidence favours correlation adjacency over geographical or hybrid priors on this dataset, while documenting a trade-off between demand-only accuracy and joint stress forecasting; attribution analysis highlights limitation and calendar coalitions and reports partial cross-method agreement. The work contributes a reproducible national-case study of graph-transformer multi-task forecasting for regional load and operational stress in Bangladesh, scoped to offline evaluation rather than universal superiority or demonstrated deployment outcomes.
\end{abstract}
